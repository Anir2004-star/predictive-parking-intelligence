APPROACH SUMMARY
----------------
Problem: Predict normalized traffic demand (0-1) for road segments
at specific geohash locations and timestamps for Day 49.

SCORE ACHIEVED: 91.54 (92.27% Accuracy)

================================================================
FEATURE ENGINEERING
================================================================

1. GEOSPATIAL FEATURES
   - Decoded geohash to latitude and longitude using pygeohash
   - Extracted geohash prefixes: gh3, gh4, gh5 (hierarchical location buckets)
   - Frequency encoding: count of how many records each geohash appears

2. TEMPORAL FEATURES
   - Extracted hour and minute from timestamp
   - Created 30-min and 15-min time bins
   - Peak hour flags: morning peak (7-10am), evening peak (5-8pm), night (10pm-5am)
   - Cyclical encoding: sin/cos of hour to capture periodicity

3. TARGET ENCODING (NO DATA LEAK)
   - Applied 10-Fold Out-Of-Fold (OOF) safe target encoding
   - Encoded features: gh5, gh6, RoadType, time_bin_15m,
     [gh5 x time_bin_30m], [gh4 x RoadType], [time_bin_30m x Weather]
   - Smoothing alpha=10 to prevent high-cardinality overfitting

4. LABEL ENCODING
   - Encoded categorical: RoadType, LargeVehicles, Landmarks,
     Weather, gh3, gh4, gh5, gh6

================================================================
MODELS USED
================================================================

Ensemble of 3 gradient boosting models trained with 10-Fold CV:

1. LightGBM
   - num_leaves=255, learning_rate=0.02
   - num_boost_round=4000, early stopping=150
   - OOF RMSE: 0.03005

2. XGBoost
   - max_depth=8, learning_rate=0.02
   - num_boost_round=4000, early stopping=150
   - OOF RMSE: 0.03032

3. CatBoost
   - depth=8, learning_rate=0.02
   - iterations=4000, early stopping=150
   - OOF RMSE: 0.03014

ENSEMBLE METHOD:
   - Used scipy.optimize.minimize to find mathematically optimal weights
   - Optimal weights: LGB=0.39, XGB=0.26, CAT=0.35
   - Final Ensemble OOF RMSE: 0.02943

PSEUDO-LABEL BLENDING:
   - Blended 70% V5 predictions + 30% V4 predictions for stability
